From The Italian (1797), Ann Radcliffe

Around 1764, some English travelers in Italy were touring the area around Naples when they came across the portico of the Santa Maria del Pianto, a church belonging to a very old convent of the order of the Black Penitents.

The magnificent portico, though damaged by time, excited so much admiration that the travelers were curious to see the structure it belonged to. They ascended the marble steps leading to it.
